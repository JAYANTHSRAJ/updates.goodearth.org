Nature Retreats, Culture & Community

May 24, 2025
Saarang is a vibrant learning ecosystem where the land itself becomes the teacher. From curious children to wise elders, every visitor engages in hands-on experiences that are sensory, expressive, and deeply rooted in nature's rhythms. Whether through playful exploration or focused training, Saarang fosters connection, creativity, and a shared sense of wonder - making every retreat more than just a memory.

Saarang Nature Retreats

Celebrating Learning Through Life, Nature Escapes at Saarang

Harvest Wisdom — A workshop for Senior Citizens

Saarang hosted a workshop rooted in sustainable agriculture and conscious living in March for senior citizens. Through hands-on organic farming sessions and engaging conversations around health, lifestyle, and sustainability, participants explored regenerative farming models practiced at Saarang.

Pennidam — A retreat for feminine wellness

Held as a residential retreat, Pennidam offered a nurturing space for reflection. Participants engaged in heartfelt conversations, and explored challenges and growth.

Set against the backdrop of riverside cookouts and campfire circles under the open sky, the retreat created a warm, grounding space for connection, insight, and healing through shared experiences.

Pachakuthira — Day and Residential Camps for Kids

This multi-day camp offered children aged 7-12 a joyful and immersive experience in nature, creativity, and culture. Through hands-on sessions in art and theatre, traditional songs, collaborative games, bird watching, and learning about sustainable food sources, the children deepened their connection to the land while celebrating the richness of local heritage.

Pupa — A teen empowerment camp

Held from May 24–25, Pupa was designed exclusively for young minds to explore creativity, confidence, and curiosity. The camp featured interactive discussions, group activities, and hands-on sessions in basic AI and robotics.

From bird watching to creative thinking exercises, teens engaged in experiences that sparked digital awareness, improved public speaking, and supported personal growth in a fun, encouraging environment.

Cultural Look Out

A 28-day temple festival held in the heart of a dense forest

The Vaishaka Mahotsavam, offers an unparalleled experience unique to Kerala. Hosted by the Kottiyoor temples-Akkare Kottiyoor and Ikkare Kottiyoor-situated on the banks of the mighty River Bavali, in Kannur district, this festival is among the most cherished in Northern Kerala. The Kottiyoor festival is deeply rooted in mythology and cultural traditions. According to legend, the temple is situated where the ancient Daksha Yaga took place.

The Akkare Kottiyoor Temple, open only during the festival, is notable for its absence of a formal structure, with the deity placed on a natural stone platform.

Steps that shape us: From Kerala to the Himalayas

Satheesh's journey began at Puralimala in Kannur - a 3,000 ft peak steeped in history and quiet strength. What started as a trek through the Western Ghats soon ignited a deep love for nature, adventure, and the discipline of walking through wild landscapes.

Drawn by the freedom and challenge of the trail, he later set his sights on the Himalayas, where the harsh, high-altitude terrain tested his limits and deepened his connection to the land, teaching him resilience, solitude, and the raw beauty of the mountains.
